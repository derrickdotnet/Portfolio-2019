Miyazaki Museum Project


The Miyazaki Museum is a personal project showcasing the films of animation director Hayao Miyazaki and his work with Studio Ghibli. This is also a study in UI design, responsive layouts, and user experience design as well.

I went in with a mobile first mindset, and it reflected in the sketches in the sketches. From here I went straight to designing a high fidelity mockup.

I created multiple versions of the desktop layout with minute changes in each. I wanted to play around with different ideas to see what they would look like in realiry. I conducted some A/B testing for the screens to see which versions people liked the most. Aftrer getting an even split of opinions, I chose to combine elements of the mobile and desktop UI for the final product.

Wireframe Sketches and Notes



It's easier to add complexity to something simple than it is to simplify something complex. From the mobile screens I created the tablet and desktop versions. I took into account the additional width that came with larger devices and incorporated a design that would utilize take advantage of the extra room.
